About Horse Events

The Intercollegiate Horse Shows Association (IHSA) is a renowned equestrian organization that provides opportunities for collegiate riders across the United States and parts of Canada to compete in horseback riding without the need for owning their own horses. Founded in 1967, IHSA has grown into one of the largest and most accessible collegiate equestrian associations, boasting over 400 member colleges and universities. It offers riders of all skill levels, from beginners to advanced, a chance to participate in a wide range of equestrian disciplines, including hunter seat equitation, western horsemanship, dressage, and more. One of IHSA's distinctive features is its catch-riding format, where riders draw horses by chance for competition, leveling the playing field and emphasizing the rider's abilities rather than the horse's quality. This unique approach promotes sportsmanship, adaptability, and the development of riders' horsemanship skills. IHSA competitions culminate in national championships where the top riders from various regions gather to compete for titles, further fostering a sense of camaraderie and excellence within the collegiate equestrian community. With its commitment to providing accessible equestrian experiences for students of diverse backgrounds and riding abilities, IHSA continues to play a vital role in shaping the future of equestrian sports in higher education.
